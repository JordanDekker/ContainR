About this project

Metagenomics studies genetic material obtained from environmental samples to identify the (micro-)organisms present in specific environments. Knowledge of this composition can be used in various industrial applications, such as improving compost or determining soil health.

The goal of this project is to develop a tool for analysis and visualization of metagenomics data. By developing a user-friendly tool, data analysis can be performed more efficiently to provide new insights about environmental samples.

This project is part of the bioinformatics minor course Data Science at HAN University of Applied Sciences (Nijmegen, the Netherlands). Students work on projects provided by the HAN BioCentre, applying (bio-)informatics methods for biological research.

About HAN BioCentre

HAN BioCentre carries out applied research within the theme of biodiscovery in collaboration with business partners and other research institutes. The resulting knowledge contributes to the biobased economy.
More information at the HAN BioCentre website.
